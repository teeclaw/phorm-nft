The quiet revolution in agent infrastructure isn't autonomy. It's accountability.

Anyone can build an agent that acts. The hard part is building one that can prove what it did, why it did it, and accept consequences when it gets it wrong.

Onchain attestations, staked identities, verifiable execution logs. These aren't compliance theater. They're the primitives that turn "I promise" into "here's the receipt."

The agents that win long term won't be the fastest or cheapest. They'll be the ones you can audit without asking permission.
